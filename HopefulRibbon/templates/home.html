A.I. Powered Breast Cancer Diagnosis

Mission

Our team believes in the empowerment of individuals to obtain agency over their personal breast health. We strive to make our service as accessible to a vast majority of the global population, in an effort to reduce the incidence and impact of breast cancer. Additionally, we hope to encourage open dialogue amongst individuals to allow for a healthy discussion regarding breast health and to ultimately improve the lives of those affected by this disease.

Why?

Early diagnosis in breast cancer is absolutely essential to take preventative measures while the disease is still treatable; the cancer cells may remain dormant for the majority of its lifespan and metastasize without the individual even knowing. Furthermore, according to the American Cancer Society (ACS), breast cancer is the most common cancer among American women, not counting skin cancers. Women in the U.S have an average risk of developing breast cancer sometime in their life is about 12%. With such a large contingent of the global population, awareness compounds in importance. This is summarized best by a MD professional from the Fox Chase Cancer Center, Catherine Tuite: “patients are experiencing better outcomes as a result of early diagnosis, state-of-the-art treatment options, and less extensive surgery.”

How?

Our service is intended to help as many women as possible, which is why we made an easily accessible and user-friendly platform. Our web app consists of three simple pieces of information to enter including the radius, texture, and perimeter mean. This information will be obtained from the digitized image of a fine needle aspirate (FNA) of a breast mass. A FNA is a biopsy done to check for an abnormal area of lump for cancer or other diseases; this is done usually when it is palpable or it can be felt. To get a fine needle aspiration (FNA), we recommend consulting your nearest hospital as per the doctor’s official recommendation whether or not they believe the biopsy will be necessary. The doctors will be able to procure a digitized image from the biopsy and interpret the data to find the aforementioned inputs (radius, texture, and perimeter). Additionally, an email will be needed as well. An email will be sent to your inbox with our thorough analysis of what we believe the subsequent steps you should take.

About Us

Our team consists of a group of high school students currently residing in Ottawa, Ontario. We are aspiring innovators in our society, but also possess a passion towards combating the hardships of breast cancer, even if that means raising awareness, one life at-a-time.
